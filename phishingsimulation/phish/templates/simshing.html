Vishing

What is Vishing?

Vishing, or voice phishing, is a type of social engineering attack where attackers use phone calls to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information. This can include personal identification information, bank account details, and passwords.

How It Works

Attackers often impersonate legitimate organizations, such as banks or government agencies, and use various tactics to instill fear or urgency in their targets. They may use spoofed phone numbers to make it appear as though the call is coming from a trusted source.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never provide personal information over the phone unless you initiated the call.
  • Verify the identity of the caller by calling back on a known number.
  • Be wary of calls that pressure you to act quickly.
  • Use call-blocking features on your phone.
  • Report any suspicious calls to your phone provider or relevant authorities.

Why Is Vishing Dangerous?

Vishing can lead to identity theft and financial loss, as attackers can gain access to sensitive information and use it for fraudulent activities. Victims may struggle to recover their identity and finances after falling victim to such attacks.
