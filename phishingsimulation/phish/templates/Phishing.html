What is Email Phishing?

Email phishing involves sending fraudulent emails that appear to be from legitimate sources, aiming to trick users into sharing personal or financial information. It is one of the most common types of cyberattacks.

How It Works

Attackers send an email posing as a trusted organization, such as a bank or service provider, asking recipients to click a link, download an attachment, or provide sensitive details like passwords, credit card information, or social security numbers.

The attackers use these details to commit fraud, steal identities, or deploy malware. These emails often look legitimate, with logos and language similar to what the recipient might expect.

Why Is Phishing Dangerous?

Phishing can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and unauthorized access to sensitive information. Even trained professionals can sometimes fall victim to cleverly disguised phishing attempts.

Organizations are increasingly adopting anti-phishing tools and training employees to recognize phishing emails, but attackers continue to evolve their techniques.
