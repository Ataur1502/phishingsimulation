Whaling

What is Whaling Phishing?

Whaling phishing is a targeted attack aimed at high-profile individuals, such as executives or key decision-makers within an organization. The goal is to deceive these targets into divulging sensitive information or authorizing fraudulent transactions.

How It Works

In whaling attacks, attackers often create highly customized messages that appear legitimate, using the victim's name and other specific details. These emails may request sensitive information or prompt the target to click on malicious links or download harmful attachments.

Attackers may also spoof the email addresses of trusted contacts to make their messages seem more credible.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Implement multi-factor authentication for sensitive accounts.
  • Train employees to recognize whaling attempts and suspicious emails.
  • Regularly update email security protocols.
  • Verify requests for sensitive information through alternate channels.
  • Monitor financial transactions closely for any irregular activity.

Why Is Whaling Dangerous?

Whaling attacks can have devastating consequences, leading to significant financial losses and breaches of sensitive information. Because these attacks target high-level individuals, they can compromise an entire organization's security if successful.
